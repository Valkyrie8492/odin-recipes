Miso Soup

The best soup, wonderfully savory and usable with almost any meal

This delicious and versatile soup only has a few ingredients needed and supplements any meal imaginable.

The soup base is easy to put together and can be drank as a savory broth or with added toppings like tofu and wakame.

Try this one especially as a warm wake-me-up in the morning for breakfast along with rice!

Ingredients

  • Kombu
  • Bonito flakes (also called Katsuobushi)
  • Dried Shiitake mushrooms(optional)
  • Toppings such as tofu, wakame, and green onions

Recipe Instructions

  1. Begin by soaking 2 2x2 squares of kombu in 1 quart of cold water for at least an hour in a stovetop pot. This is best done overnight beforehand and can be scaled
  2. When it is time to make the dashi, bring the kombu and water up to a simmer on a medium heat. Remove the kombu from the water before it begins to boil
  3. Add 3-5 Shiitake mushrooms and 10g of bonito flakes for each quart of water and keep them on a steamy heat for about 10-15 minutes
  4. Turn off the heat and strain the mixture. The liquid is your dashi and will be the base of your soup; it can be stored in the fridge for ~5 days safely
  5. To make the soup get the hot dashi broth to a steamy but not boiling heat and add about a spoonful of miso paste per bowl. you can add more to taste depending on saltiness and how light you like the soup.
  6. Add the other toppings you desire, common ones include cubed tofu, wakame that's soaked in the soup for a few minutes, and green onions. Serve as a side to your favorite Japanese inspired meal for a warming, comforting side soup!
